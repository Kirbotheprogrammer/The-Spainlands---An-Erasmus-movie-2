Alas, unlike other times, I don’t have much to say, but I will guide you step by step behind the scenes.

Let’s start with the naming of the An Erasmus Movie films.

The number is NOT random: it literally indicates the number of the film I worked on. If another film is made in the future, I would call it An Erasmus Movie 3, regardless of anything else.

For me, what really matters is the second title:
Erasmus Crusaders… "Erasmus Crusaders” is a playful reference to “Stardust Crusaders,” the third part of the manga/anime JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure by Hirohiko Araki.
The SpainLands…

When I talk about The SpainLands, I’m referring to An Erasmus Movie 2, and vice versa.

The only exception would be a possible The SpainLands 2, which would indicate additional content: in that case, the movie number would remain unchanged.
For example, if there were a The SpainLands 9, it would still be An Erasmus Movie 2.

You can think of An Erasmus Movie 2 as a full-fledged universe, and The SpainLands as its chapters. I know it may seem complicated: that’s why we created the website.

As for the interviews in the video… did you know they were chosen at random, while we were in the car? Funny, right? This connects to what I wrote in “Revisiting a Great Classic.” I believe looking back with detachment helps me grow creatively while embracing change. True creativity comes from daring, isolating myself, and drawing inspiration from others. Unlike A.I., I can imagine and take risks. Revisiting classics isn’t copying, it’s planting seeds for my own growth.
